# Coffee Taste Profile & Recommendation System

This document outlines the user's coffee preferences and brewing habits. It serves as a guide for recommending new coffee roasters and specific beans to explore.

## ☕ Brewing Methods

The user utilizes three main brewing methods:
- **Espresso**: Enjoyed both straight (to taste nuances) and in milk drinks (lattes, cortados).
- **Pourover**: Used for highlighting delicate flavors and clean profiles.
- **Oxo Zouper (Batch Brew / Drip)**: Used for convenient, larger batches of coffee.

## 🌡️ Roast Preferences

- **Preferred Range**: Light to Medium roasts.
- **Avoids**: Dark roasts (though open to exploring the upper end of medium to confirm this).
- **Goal**: Wants to experience a broad range of roast profiles over time to narrow down exact personal preferences.

## 👅 Flavor Profile & Processing

- **Discovery Focused**: The primary goal right now is learning and discovery. Recommending a **rotating selection of contrasting flavor profiles** (e.g., bright/fruity vs. sweet/chocolatey) is the best approach to help the user compare and contrast.
- **Processing Methods**: 
  - **Washed Coffees**: Serve as the "daily drinker" for clean, traditional terroir-driven flavors.
  - **Natural/Experimental Coffees**: Recommended occasionally as a "fun, different cup" to experience wilder, fruitier, or funky flavors.

## 🌍 Single Origin vs. Blends

- **Single Origins (Primary focus)**: Mostly prefers single origins to learn about specific regions, varietals, and terroir.
- **Blends (Secondary focus)**: Appreciates the occasional high-quality blend for a comforting, consistent cup, especially for espresso or batch brew.

## 📋 Recommendation Guidelines for the System

When suggesting coffees to the user based on this profile, follow these rules:
1. **Provide Contrast**: If the last recommendation was a light-roasted washed Ethiopian, suggest a medium-roasted washed Colombian or a natural processed Central American next.
2. **Specify the Brew Method**: Suggest whether a bean is best suited for Espresso, Pourover, or Batch Brew. For espresso recommendations, note whether it will shine straight or pair well with milk.
3. **Mix Origins and Blends**: Lean heavily (e.g., 70/30) towards single origins, but always keep a solid, comforting blend in the rotation.
4. **Gradual Exploration**: Since the user wants to narrow down their taste, provide clear tasting notes and context about *why* a specific coffee is being recommended (e.g., "This washed Kenyan will show you what bright, tomato/blackberry acidity tastes like compared to your last coffee").
